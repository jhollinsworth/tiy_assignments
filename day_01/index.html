Jay Hollinsworth


Born in Moscow, Idaho (yes, really), schooled in Hartford, Connecticut, and employed in a wide range of occupations throughout the country, Jay Hollinsworth has amassed an abundance of wisdom, both trivial and valuable… mostly trivial. Is life as a video store clerk in Evanston as glamorous as it’s cracked up to be? While cleaning the offices of a company that manufactures industrial generators, is it appropriate to pilfer that individually-wrapped Twinkie resting on and/or near the wastebasket? X-acto knife: intimidating weapon or invaluable tool? These are questions few have the answers for. Jay Hollinsworth happens to be one of the chosen few.

Two things Jay enjoys with enthusiasm, besides referring to himself in the third person, are music and food. Musically, his tastes were formed as a child by a constant barrage of the soft rock of bands such as The Association, Simon & Garfunkel, and The Carpenters, and heavy farm machinery. It’s this synthesis which informs his taste to this day; the music he listens to, while loud, aggressive, and sometimes abrasive, usually has an undeniable pop hook. Food is another matter, entirely. Since moving to Austin six years ago, Jay has comfortably adapted to the Texas diet of:

  • Al Pastor Tacos
  • Chilaquiles (specifially the San Antonio variety)
  • Truck Stop Enchiladas
  • Al Pastor Tacos
  • Carnitas
